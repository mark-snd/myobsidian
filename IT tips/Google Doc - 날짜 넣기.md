Yes, there are several ways to add a date to your Google Doc using a shortcut! Here are three options:
 
1. Inserting "today's date":

- Keyboard shortcut: Type **@today** and then press Enter. This will automatically insert the current date.

2. Inserting specific dates:

- Keyboard shortcut: Type **@date** and then type the specific date you want to insert. For example, you could type **@3/15/2024** to insert March 15th, 2024.

3. Inserting dynamic dates (yesterday, tomorrow):

- Keyboard shortcuts:
    - **@yesterday**: Inserts the date of yesterday.
    - @tomorrow: Inserts the date of tomorrow.

Bonus tip:

- You can also use the Insert menu, though shortcuts are faster:
    - Go to Insert \> Smart Chips \> Date
    - Choose the desired date format and click "Insert."
      
    
 
Remember, these methods will only insert the date once. If you want the date to automatically update to the current date whenever you open the document, you can use Google Apps Script, but that's a more advanced option.
